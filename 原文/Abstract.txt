Abstract
As a naturally abundant renewable aromatic polymer, lignin holds great potential for high-value utilization, which is significant for achieving carbon peak and neutrality goals. However, its inherent structural complexity and broad molecular weight distribution cause unstable performance, hindering high-end applications. Ultrafiltration fractionation can purify lignin into more homogeneous fractions by molecular size, yet its influence on the subsequent formation of colloidal spheres and pyrolysis kinetics remains poorly understood. In this study, industrial alkali lignin was fractionated into four fractions (>100 kDa, 20–100 kDa, 10–20 kDa, and 5–10 kDa), and their pyrolysis behavior and colloidal sphere formation were systematically investigated. Characterization showed that ultrafiltration markedly narrowed the molecular weight distribution, reduced the polydispersity index, and induced regular structural changes. Colloidal sphere studies revealed that molecular weight governs particle morphology and size: low-molecular-weight lignin formed small (~100–300 nm), highly spherical spheres, while high-molecular-weight lignin produced larger (~1–2 μm), rough particles. The medium-to-low molecular weight fraction performed best in uniformity and stability, yielding 400–800 nm spheres promising for drug carriers and cosmetic additives. Pyrolysis kinetics analysis using the Coats–Redfern method showed that higher molecular weight leads to greater structural stability, higher energy barriers, and delayed reaction progress. This work clarifies the relationships among ultrafiltration fractionation, lignin structure, pyrolysis behavior, and colloidal sphere formation, and proposes an optimal strategy: ultrafiltration and homogenization to obtain fractions with low polydispersity and medium-to-low molecular weight, followed by hydrothermal self-organization to produce uniform, highly spherical colloids. The findings offer a theoretical basis and new approach for precise fractionation and directional high-value conversion of lignin.

Keywords: Lignin; Ultrafiltration fractionation; Pyrolysis; Colloidal sphere; Biomass resource utilization
